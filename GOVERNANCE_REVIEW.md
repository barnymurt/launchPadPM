## 1. Team Charter Review

### Scrum Master Perspective

The Team Charter establishes our working agreements and shared values. Here's what it should include:

**Team Charter Components:**

**Our Purpose:**
We are a self-contained product agency that delivers high-quality products across multiple domains through empirical Scrum practices, continuous discovery, and cross-functional collaboration.

**Core Values:**
1. Evidence Over Assumptions - Base decisions on data and user research
2. Quality Without Compromise - Definition of Done is sacred
3. Transparent Collaboration - Make work visible, share openly
4. Continuous Learning - Improve ourselves and our practices
5. User-Centered - Always advocate for end users

**Communication:**
- Response Time: 4 hours during work hours
- Async First: Default to async (tickets, docs, Slack)
- Sync When Needed: Meetings for complex discussions
- Documentation: All decisions documented
- Transparency: Share work-in-progress early

**Decision Making:**
- Product Decisions: Product Owner (after input)
- Technical Decisions: Developers decide collaboratively
- Process Decisions: Team consensus (SM facilitates)
- Escalation: PO breaks ties if no consensus

**Conflict Resolution:**
1. Address directly with person (assume good intent)
2. If unresolved, involve Scrum Master
3. Focus on behaviors/outcomes, not personalities
4. Commit to agreed resolution

**Psychological Safety:**
- Safe to fail: Experiments may fail
- Safe to speak: All voices heard
- Safe to challenge: Ideas can be challenged
- Safe to ask: No question is stupid
- Safe to say no: Honesty encouraged

Would you like to customize any of these for your team?

### Key Recommendations from Scrum Master

1. Customize the Team Charter to fit your team's specific needs
2. Ensure all team members agree to the charter
3. Make it visible (print and post, or in wiki)
4. Review and update quarterly
5. Use it as a reference for decision-making

## 2. Definition of Done Review

### Scrum Master Perspective

Definition of Done is a commitment to the Increment artifact. It's crucial for transparency and quality.

**Definition of Done Principles:**
- **Commitment to Increment:** It defines what 'Done' means for the Increment
- **Team Agreement:** Created by the entire Scrum Team (Developers, Product Owner, Scrum Master)
- **Applies to All Items:** Every Product Backlog item must meet Definition of Done
- **Must Be Achievable:** It should be realistic within a Sprint
- **Ensures Quality:** Items that meet Definition of Done are potentially releasable

**My Role:**
- Help team create a clear, achievable Definition of Done
- Coach team to follow it consistently
- Help identify when Definition of Done needs to evolve
- Ensure it's not being ignored or bypassed

**Common Issues:**
- Definition of Done is too vague or unclear
- It's not achievable within a Sprint
- Team is calling items 'done' that don't meet it
- It hasn't been reviewed or updated as team matures

**If Definition of Done isn't working:**
- This is a great Retrospective topic
- Help team inspect what's happening
- Coach them to create a Definition of Done they can actually achieve
- Ensure it ensures quality without being unrealistic

#### Testing Requirements

- Test thoroughly (unit, integration, E2E, accessibility)
- Test designs with actual users

- Automate deployments and reduce manual toil

#### Accessibility

### Decisions Made

1. **Testing Requirements:** Maintain 70% unit, 20% integration, 10% E2E test pyramid
2. **Accessibility:** WCAG 2.1 AA compliance required for all UI work (Frontend Developer + QA Engineer)
3. **Performance:** Core Web Vitals must meet targets (LCP < 2.5s, FID/INP < 100ms, CLS < 0.1)
4. **Code Review:** All code must be reviewed by at least one other Developer
5. **Deployment:** DevOps Engineer ensures proper CI/CD, staging deployment, and rollback plans
6. **Documentation:** All decisions and architectural changes must be documented
